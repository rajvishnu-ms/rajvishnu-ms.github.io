Dear Hiring Team,

I am applying for the Vehicle Systems Engineer position at Humble Robotics. Building an autonomous, zero-emissions hauler means making complex mechanical, electrical and software subsystems work as one reliable platform, and that system-level integration and validation is exactly what I have done for the past 8+ years.

At TDK Sensors AG I led the integration of cross-discipline subsystems across mechanical, electrical and software domains, defining system architectures and interface definitions and managing the interfaces between sensor, control-unit and software subsystems. I planned and executed system-level and HIL validation, building hardware-in-the-loop benches that combined real control units with virtual subsystem models to verify behaviour against product requirements and safety goals. I performed system-level analysis of performance, reliability and fault tolerance, identified integration risks and implemented mitigations, supported design reviews to keep subsystem teams aligned, and drove root-cause analysis of system-level issues using oscilloscopes, logic analysers and protocol decoders.

I work fluently with vehicle communication protocols including CAN and Ethernet, use MATLAB/Simulink for system modelling, and apply functional-safety practices (ISO 26262) and requirements traceability as a matter of course. My earlier work reinforces the cross-disciplinary core of this role: at KIT I developed and tested control software on a robotic electromechanical platform, and at Bosch I specified and coordinated critical components with suppliers and led engineering-change impact analysis across assemblies and processes.

I have a strong drive to quickly learn complex new systems, I am comfortable in a fast-moving startup environment, and I take ownership early. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my systems integration and validation experience can help Humble Robotics bring its platform from concept to reliable deployment.
