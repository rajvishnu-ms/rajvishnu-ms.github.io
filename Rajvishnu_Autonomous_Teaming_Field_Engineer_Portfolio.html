Technical Deep Dive

Interview-ready answers

How would you prepare a live autonomous-system demonstration?

I would convert the mission into a controlled configuration: define the environment, interfaces, expected behavior, safety limits, operator roles, checkpoints, logging plan, spares and abort criteria. I would run a dry test using the same hardware and configuration, freeze versions, and ensure every critical observation can be correlated after the event.

How would you troubleshoot a detection failure in the field?

First verify whether the failure is perception, timing, configuration, communication, power or platform related. Preserve raw sensor data and logs, confirm timestamps and coordinate assumptions, compare against a known target and baseline, and change only one variable at a time. The immediate goal is a safe working setup; the second goal is evidence for a permanent fix.

How does automotive validation transfer to counter-UAS?

Both require deterministic interfaces, timing awareness, hardware-near debugging, safe fault behavior, traceable configurations and evidence-based release decisions. My automotive background brings disciplined integration and verification methods; my robotics and mechanical experience adds the real physical-system perspective.
