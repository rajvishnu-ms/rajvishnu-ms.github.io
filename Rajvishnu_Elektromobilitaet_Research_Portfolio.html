Proposed THD Research Project

Data-driven optimization of regional electromobility and charging infrastructure

This proposed research concept is built from my existing automotive, sensor, data-analysis and project-coordination experience and is clearly separated from completed professional work.

Main research question

How can vehicle, infrastructure and mobility-demand data be combined to identify technically, economically and environmentally robust electromobility strategies for regional transport systems?

Proposed research

Research problem

Regional electromobility systems must balance vehicle energy demand, charging availability, infrastructure cost, grid constraints, accessibility and user requirements. These factors interact strongly and can lead to conflicting decisions.

Sub-questions

  • Which vehicle, route and operating characteristics have the greatest influence on energy demand?
  • How does charging location influence availability, utilization and service reliability?
  • Which charging concepts provide the best balance between cost, grid impact and user needs?
  • How can private EVs, public transport and shared mobility be compared transparently?
  • How sensitive are recommendations to electricity mix, demand profiles and regional constraints?

Methodology

  • Literature and technology review
  • Python/MATLAB-based data preparation and modelling
  • Scenario analysis and multicriteria decision analysis
  • Sensitivity and uncertainty analysis
  • Design of experiments and KPI development
  • Validation using measured, public or synthetic datasets
  • Stakeholder workshops and industry feedback

Required datasets

  • Vehicle energy consumption and duty cycles
  • Route, trip-demand and utilization profiles
  • Charging events and station availability
  • Electricity tariffs and grid constraints
  • Public-transport schedules and accessibility
  • Emission factors and infrastructure cost

Research discussion topics

How would you develop a new research proposal?

I would begin with a trend and stakeholder map, identify a measurable research gap, define impact-oriented objectives and KPIs, structure work packages, identify data and validation needs, establish partner responsibilities and align the proposal with the target funding program.

How does automotive validation transfer to transport research?

Both require clear assumptions, traceable requirements, representative scenarios, controlled data collection, uncertainty assessment and reproducible evidence. My experience adds discipline in turning complex system behavior into measurable and decision-ready results.

What can you contribute to teaching?

Hands-on examples in automotive sensors, data analysis, validation, project coordination, MCDA, root-cause methods and industry product development, supported by structured exercises and reproducible datasets.
